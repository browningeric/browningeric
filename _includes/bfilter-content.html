What are we building?
How long is it going to take?
How much is it going to cost?

These are questions software development lifecycle (SDLC) leaders struggle to answer. Bloomfilter uses process mining, AI, and real data from product and engineering tools to paint a clear picture of the actual processes and helps teams understand how to reduce waste.

Initial scope, research, and insights

When I started with Bloomfilter the platform was essentially a proof of concept. The general idea resonated with early customers but UX had not been a priority and there were no design standards in place.

We needed to understand where we should go next.

What features or solutions would solve the biggest problems for the most people?

Interviewees

  • CTOs
  • ENG Managers
  • Product Executives

New Feature: Strategic Priorities

One thing users repeatedly asked for was an ability to define high level plans and goals, so that the granular data Bloomfilter presented could be tied back to those for tracking, performance reviews, and resource planning.

The scope was kept narrow and focused with the understanding further integrations and enhancements would add more capability over time.

What was the most basic unit we could ask a user to define? What was the most impactful data they would see right away?

A View into Financials

Another key challenge I heard was how to bridge the gap between engineering deliverables and intervals (cards, points, sprints, etc) and those used by the organization at large (dollars, quarters, etc).

I prototyped a view focused on using aggregate values combined with data in the platform.

This didn't work for users as aggregate values were inaccurate and the view still required looking at various different contexts and piecing them together.

Iterating based on Feedback

I redesigned a flow based on division, where users entered in spend amounts and calculations were made based on that. The primary visualization was a multi-axis graph of arbitrary time series data. Although it initially felt complex to me it was very well received in testing, offering a flexible and insightful view.

Design System

While building out these features, I worked closely with our engineering team to begin defining and implementing a basic design system. I started very tactically, cleaning up and presenting a color palette, basic text styling, and common UI controls like buttons. I took care to document the why? for decisions to support growth and expansion.

We set up a Storybook instance and began to tie in common settings and values as design tokens.

Results

These features and the design system are helping Bloomfilter launch their platform.
