CraftyCloud

Making life easier for craft fair organizers

The Challenge

Running craft fairs is challenging, and the process of managing vendor applications and approvals is one of the most time-consuming and cumbersome parts. I worked with a panel of organizers to plan and prototype a system that would make the process simple for them, and maybe even a little fun.

Getting Started

I started the project focused on listening and understanding. I interviewed organizers to identify pain points and challenges. We then collaborated on sketching some user flows and feature ideas.

I refined the sketches and incorporated some additional feedback to create a more polished version of this, which was valuable in discussions with remote team members; it's easier for some people to connect with a clean deliverable than trying to interpret sketches.

Design Iteration

For major components, I used a quick feedback loop approach. I tested various layout options with the team, then the best choices were tested in another round with different content, and a third round if necessary. This allowed quick progress but still considered different lengths and types of content.

Results

Once we had enough of a product to test, we put it in front of craft fair organizers and vendors, and used surveys to learn more. Unfortunately, we found there wasn't a great market fit; peolpe loved the idea and the feature set, but the price point they could support (and the overall market size) didn't make sense to continue building and launching the app.

What I Learned

Since the end product was an administrative interface to customize an API, there were many places a user could get overwhelmed by options. Unfortunately, these were necessary; I had to brainstorm ways to present forms and tables with a high information density while also finding places to add visual interest and branding.
